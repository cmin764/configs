# Safety Zones Reference

> **Coverage:** Vetted on the ground for Colombia and Ecuador (2025-2026).
> For other countries, add a section below using the same Green/Amber/Red
> structure after each trip. Fall back on general knowledge until local data exists.

## General rule (Latin America, especially Colombia)
Higher on the hill = higher risk. The hillside gradient is the single most
reliable proxy for safety when you don't know a specific neighborhood.
Cable car stations and their immediate surroundings are exceptions — they
tend to be slightly safer than the surrounding hillside.

## Medellín

### Green — base here without hesitation
- El Poblado (Manila sector preferred over Parque Lleras zone)
- Laureles
- Envigado (flat central area, near Parque Envigado)
- Sabaneta

### Amber — verify specific address before accepting
- Belén (lower flat part only — Belén La Mota fine, Belén Rincón uphill problematic)
- Buenos Aires (lower streets fine, hillside above Av. El Poblado avoid)
- Itagüí (some residential pockets OK, check address specifically)
- Eastern Envigado hillside (toward Las Palmas / El Peñasco — safe but transport friction)

### Red — do not stay, visit only on guided day tours if at all
- Doce de Octubre
- Upper Robledo
- San Javier (Metrocable passes through — ride is fine in daylight, don't stay)
- El Centro / Downtown (day visits OK with guide, never stay)
- Barrio Trinidad, Aranjuez, Prado, Santo Domingo (north of center)

### Transport note
Uber and DiDi work city-wide. InDrive available. Avoid street taxis at night.
UberYa (scheduled) for airport runs and early day trip departures.

## Cartagena

### Green — base here
- Getsemaní (improved significantly, authentic neighborhood feel)
- El Centro / Old Town (expensive but safe, tourist police presence)
- Bocagrande (beach zone, safe, more generic)

### Red — avoid entirely
- Torices
- Canapote
- Bazurto market area surroundings at night

### Transport note
InDrive recommended by locals, minimum 8,000 COP.
Negotiate fixed fare with airport taxis before getting in.
Mototaxis for short hops inside the old city.

## Bogotá

### Green
- Chapinero (Zona G, Chapinero Alto, Quinta Camacho)
- Usaquén
- Zona Rosa / Parque 93 area
- Rosales

### Amber
- La Candelaria (day visits with guide, don't stay)
- Santa Fe adjacent streets

### Red
- Downtown south of La Candelaria at night
- Kennedy, Bosa, Ciudad Bolívar

## Santa Marta

### Green
- El Rodadero (beach zone, safe)
- Historic center (Parque de los Novios area)
- Near SMR airport (for easy exit day)

### Amber
- Mercado Público surroundings (fine for buses and market, don't linger at night)

### Transport note
InDrive and Uber both work. Taxis from airport: negotiate before getting in.
Bus to Tayrona (El Zaino): departs from Mercado Público, 15k COP/person.

## Quito (Ecuador)

### Green
- La Floresta
- González Suárez / Mariscal area (Mariscal itself has nightlife/tourist density)
- Cumbayá (suburban, quieter)

### Amber
- Mariscal (Gringo-heavy, some petty crime at night)
- La Ronda at night

### Red
- La Mariscal surroundings late at night on foot
- Centro Histórico at night without guide

## General rules across all cities

1. Phone in back pocket = invitation. Never.
2. Camera on strap in crowded areas = target. Bag it when not shooting.
3. App-based transport over street hails, always.
4. ATMs: use during daylight, inside malls or supermarkets when possible.
   Never at isolated street ATMs at night.
5. "Friendly stranger" approaches in tourist zones: high scam probability.
   Scopolamine (devil's breath) exists in Colombia. Don't accept drinks from strangers.
