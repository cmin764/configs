# ATM Fees Reference

> **Coverage:** Vetted data for Colombia and Ecuador (2025-2026).
> For other countries, add a section below using the same ranked-by-fee
> table format after each trip. General ATM strategy at the bottom applies everywhere.

## The rule that never changes
Withdraw large amounts infrequently. A 22,000 COP flat fee on 800k COP is
2.75%. The same fee on 200k COP is 11%. The math punishes small withdrawals
regardless of bank.

Always: decline DCC (dynamic currency conversion). Always pay in local currency.
Always check the screen for a surcharge warning before confirming.

## Colombia

### Ranked by foreign card surcharge (lowest to highest)

| Bank          | Fee (COP)    | Notes                                          |
|---------------|--------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Banco Popular | ~10,500      | Lowest reported. Limited branch network.       |
| Coomeva       | ~12,000      | Best widely-available option. Use this.        |
| Caja Social   | ~14,000-19,000 | Some branches reported zero fee in 2025.      |
| Colpatria/Citi | ~20,900     | Acceptable fallback.                           |
| Bancolombia   | ~22,000-27,000 | Ubiquitous, higher fee.                       |
| Servibanca    | ~22,500-28,500 | Redeban network. Common in small towns.       |
| Banco Bogotá  | ~25,800      | Aval group. Avoid if Coomeva nearby.          |

| Itaú (Aval)   | ~28,500      | Avoid.                                        |

### City-specific guidance

**Medellín (El Poblado / Laureles)**
Best: Coomeva/Bancoomeva branches (~12k COP)
Fallback: Caja Social Laureles (historically low/zero)
Avoid: BBVA, Davivienda

**Cartagena**
Best: Coomeva (~12k COP, reported Jan 2026 Bocagrande)
Fallback: Caja Social (~18k COP)
Avoid: Servibanca in tourist areas (28.5k)

**Small towns (Salento, Filandia, etc.)**
Often only Davivienda available (26k COP, sometimes hidden).
Strategy: withdraw enough cash in Pereira/Armenia before heading to villages.

**Galápagos / remote areas**
Isabela ATMs frequently empty. Santa Cruz more reliable.
Strategy: arrive with USD cash. Budget for cash-only everything.

### Revolut/Wise Colombia rules (2026)
- Revolut Standard: £200/month fee-free (5 withdrawals), then 2%
- Wise: 2 free withdrawals up to €200/month, then 2% + ~$1.50 fixed
- Neither has partner network in Colombia — all ATMs are "out of network"
- Both still face the local bank surcharge on top of their own fees

## Ecuador

### General
ATMs less standardized than Colombia. Banco Pichincha and Banco Guayaquil
most widespread. Fees typically $1.50-3.50 USD per withdrawal for foreign cards.

### Galápagos specifically
- Santa Cruz (Puerto Ayora): ATMs available, Banco del Pacífico most reliable
- Isabela (Puerto Villamil): ATMs frequently empty or out of service
- San Cristóbal: decent ATM access
- Rule: arrive at each island with enough cash for your entire stay there

Cash is essential for: ferry tickets, guided tours, restaurants, accommodation
in Isabela. Card acceptance is inconsistent even where machines exist.

---

## General ATM strategy

1. Find Coomeva or Caja Social first. Walk past everything else.
2. Withdraw the maximum the ATM allows per transaction (usually 600k-2M COP).
3. Check the screen before confirming — some banks (Davivienda) don't display
   the fee until after.
4. If the fee shown is over 20k COP, cancel and find a different ATM.
5. Keep ATM receipts. Compare against app balance within 24h.
6. Nequi (Colombia mobile wallet) is useful for small local payments and
   avoiding ATMs entirely for day-to-day spending in cities.
