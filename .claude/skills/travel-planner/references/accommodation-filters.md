# Accommodation Filters Reference

## Hard filters — instant disqualification

These are non-negotiable. If any apply, skip the listing entirely.

- Name or description contains: hostal, hostel, backpacker, dorm, shared room,
  bunk, party, social space, common area sleeping
- No private bathroom
- No private entrance (shared entrance with host family = amber, shared
  entrance with random guests = red)
- Located in a red safety zone (see safety-zones.md)
- Fewer than 3 nights minimum for a stay that needs 3+ nights
- Calendar not updated in 30+ days (likely inactive host)
- WiFi described as "basic" or not mentioned at all (for remote work stays)

## WiFi protocol for remote work trips

This is the single most important filter after safety. Apply it every time.

For any stay in a remote or semi-urban area:
- Message host: "Do you have Starlink satellite internet?"
- Request upload speed: minimum 5 Mbps upload for stable video calls
- For urban areas (Medellín, Bogotá, Quito): fiber is common, still verify
- Never assume because a listing says "WiFi available"

Starlink is the gold standard for rural/island locations. If they don't have
it, ask for a speed test screenshot. No screenshot = treat as unknown.

## Strong preferences — weighted in ranking

**Space**
- 2 people need separate work and sleep spaces
- Balcony or outdoor area for morning coffee/work is a significant plus
- 80m² minimum comfortable for 5+ nights; 60m² workable for 3 nights
- Natural light in the main room is not optional

**Host track record**
- 10+ verified reviews: high confidence
- 5-9 reviews: acceptable, read carefully
- Under 5 reviews: flag gap, requires more due diligence
- "New member" badge: ask for references or video call option
- Multiple long-stay reviews (7+ days): strongest signal of livability

**Neighborhood position**
- Walking distance to a supermarket
- Within 15 minutes of main transport axis (metro, main road, bus stop)
- Not on a hillside above the safer flat zone
- Avoid the immediate party-zone / nightlife nucleus of any city (the specific area varies — research per destination; noise bleeds in regardless of how the listing describes itself)

**Cleaning fee logic**
- High cleaning fee relative to stay length = negotiate or skip
- For 5 nights: $25-40 USD reasonable; $100+ is a red flag unless the space
  is genuinely 200m²+
- Always negotiate if you're only using one of multiple bedrooms

**Host behavior signals**
- Responds within 24h: minimum bar
- Pre-approves without asking clarifying questions: mild concern (not engaged)
- Offers to adjust terms, waives fees proactively: trust signal
- Sends a detailed welcome guide before arrival: high trust signal

## HomeExchange-specific checklist

- [ ] Loyalty badge (years of membership shown): 2+ years preferred
- [ ] ID and proof of address verified
- [ ] Telephone verified
- [ ] Profile photo and description feel genuine, not AI-generated
- [ ] Reviews mention specific details (neighborhood, kitchen, noise level)
- [ ] Calendar recently updated (within 2 weeks)
- [ ] Exchange type accepted (GuestPoints or reciprocal) — confirm before messaging
- [ ] Cleaning fee stated upfront — clarify in first message

## Questions to always ask a host before confirming

1. "Can you confirm the WiFi upload speed?" (or Starlink if rural)
2. "Which sub-neighborhood or street is the apartment on?" (for safety verification)
3. "Is there [cat/dog/other pet] on the premises during our stay?" (allergy risk)
4. "What is the nearest metro station / bus stop?"
5. For multi-bedroom homes with one couple: "Can the cleaning fee be adjusted
   since we'll only use one bedroom and bathroom?"

## Tourist tax exemptions

> Vetted examples below. Check for equivalents when planning trips to new countries.

**Colombia — IVA (19%):** Tourists with a passport entry stamp are legally exempt.
1. Show passport with immigration entry stamp at check-in
2. Ask explicitly: "I'd like the IVA exemption for tourists"
3. OTAs (Booking.com, Airbnb) often apply it by default — check invoice
4. Direct bookings easier to remove than OTA bookings
5. Not all properties know this or will honor it — press once, then drop it
